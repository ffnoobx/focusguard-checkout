Privacy Policy — FocusGuard

Last updated: June 2026

FocusGuard ("we", "our", "the extension") is a Chrome extension that helps users block distracting websites. This policy explains what information we collect and how it's used.

What we collect

FocusGuard stores your settings (blocked sites, timer preferences, theme) locally in your browser using Chrome's storage API. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to us.

If you choose to upgrade to FocusGuard Pro, you'll be asked for an email address during checkout. This email is used solely to help you get support or cancel your subscription if needed. It is processed through our payment partner, Razorpay, and is not sold or shared with any third party for marketing purposes.

If you submit feedback through our in-app feedback form, that message (and the optional email you provide) is sent to us via Formspree, a third-party form service, so we can read and respond to it.

Payments

All payments are processed securely by Razorpay. We do not store or have access to your card details, UPI ID, or any other payment credentials.

Third-party services we use

  • Razorpay — payment processing
  • Formspree — feedback form delivery
  • GitHub Pages — hosting this policy and our checkout page

Data retention

We do not run our own servers or databases. Any data shared with the third parties above is retained according to their own privacy policies.

Children's privacy

FocusGuard is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page.
